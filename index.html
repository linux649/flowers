Cherry Blossoms

The cherry blossom is the flower that grows on wild and ornamental cherry trees. Wild cherry trees are distributed in many places, but most wild cherry trees originate in the Northern Hemisphere. Many cherry trees bloom during March and April, in spring, in the Northern Hemisphere. Cherry trees in the Southern Hemisphere generally bloom during September and October, in spring. However, there are many different species of cherry trees and there may be some variation of blooming times of different species of cherry trees.

Culinary Use

Sometimes cherry blossoms and cherry leaves are used in food, after first pickling the flowers and/or leaves in salt and ume vinegar. Pickling the flowers are important because in its raw state, cherry leaves and flowers have coumarin, which is a chemical compound that is hepatotoxic (toxic to the liver) in high doses, and pickling the cherry leaves/flowers in this way reduces the amount of coumarin in the cherry leaves/flowers to safe levels. It has a vanilla scent and sweet taste. Water or tea with pickled cherry blossoms or leaves may be used for some occasions instead of green tea.

Hanami and Cherry Blossoms In Japan

Cherry blossoms (Japanese: さくら or 桜 or 櫻 or サクラ) are the national flower of Japan. There is a centuries old flower viewing tradition in Japan called hanami (Japanese: はなみ), which is to go under cherry blossom or ume (Japanese: 梅 or うめ) trees and hold parties or feasts under the trees and drink rice wine or other alcohol, while admiring or viewing the trees. Most of the cherry blossom trees in Japan are the Yoshino cherry tree variety. This specific variety can mutate easily, so some people crossbreed different varieties of Yoshino cherry to look different (increase petal number, change shade of the flower, etc).
